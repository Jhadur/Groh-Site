News


JULY 23, 2007

ASHEVILLE, N.C. — Dr. Gordon Groh, an orthopaedic surgeon at Blue Ridge Bone & Joint, recently gave a presentation at the Global Shoulder Society’s annual meeting in Toronto. Shoulder surgeons from around the world attended the event, as Groh addressed current treatment regimens for shoulder fractures and moderated a session on shoulder arthritis.

Groh presented “Reduction Techniques for Complicated Fractures” during the meeting, which included sessions on four major topics: arthroscopy, arthritis, fracture and rotator cuff repair.

“The Global Shoulder Society brings together the top shoulder surgeons from Europe, Asia and the Americas,” says Groh, who joined Asheville, N.C.-based Blue Ridge Bone & Joint in 1995. “I am honored to be able to participate in this select group that focuses on delivering the latest technology and current treatment regimens to our patients.”

The Global Shoulder Society is an international organization, with membership by invitation only, that serves as a springboard for looking at new ideas related to shoulder treatment. At the annual meetings, shoulder surgeons present data and discuss ideas related to the entire spectrum of shoulder issues – arthritis (joint replacement, synthetic substitutions, etc.), rotator cuff tears (technique, arthroscopic management, tissue scaffolds), fractures of the shoulder and treatment, and shoulder instability.

A specialist in shoulder, elbow and hand surgery, Groh is a graduate of Washington University in St. Louis. He received his medical degree from the University of Missouri School of Medicine-Columbia and completed a shoulder fellowship at the University of Texas at San Antonio, as well as a hand surgery fellowship at Baylor College of Medicine in Houston, Texas.

Groh has been awarded a certificate of added qualification in hand surgery, and he is certified by the American Board of Orthopaedic Surgery. He is a member of the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons, the American Shoulder and Elbow Society, the American Society for Surgery of the Hand, the North Carolina Orthopaedic Society and the North Carolina Hand Society.

Groh has delivered numerous research presentations on a variety of orthopaedic surgery topics and has contributed to dozens of orthopaedic-related publications. His surgical experience includes a large volume of shoulder, elbow and hand fractures each year.
